Filing in the District of Kansas

Educational reference covering the U.S. Bankruptcy Court for the District of Kansas: divisional court locations in Wichita, Topeka, and Kansas City; statutory filing fees under 28 U.S.C. § 1930; and the local rules and electronic-filing procedures that govern petition intake.

Jurisdiction and venue

The U.S. Bankruptcy Court for the District of Kansas (D. Kan., docket abbreviation ksb) is one of 90 bankruptcy court districts in the United States. It is a unit of the U.S. District Court for the District of Kansas, exercising jurisdiction under 28 U.S.C. § 157. Venue for a voluntary bankruptcy petition is governed by 28 U.S.C. § 1408, which generally requires the debtor to file in the district where the debtor's domicile, residence, principal place of business, or principal assets have been located for the 180 days preceding filing.

Court website: ksb.uscourts.gov.

Divisional court locations

The District of Kansas is a single judicial district covering all 105 Kansas counties. The bankruptcy court holds proceedings at three divisional offices:

Wichita Division (headquarters)

U.S. Courthouse, 401 N. Market Street, Wichita, KS 67202. Clerk's office for the District of Kansas bankruptcy court is located here.

Topeka Division

Frank Carlson Federal Building, 444 SE Quincy Street, Topeka, KS 66683.

Kansas City Division

Robert J. Dole U.S. Courthouse, 500 State Avenue, Kansas City, KS 66101.

Always confirm hearing locations on the court's docket entry or hearing notice; some matters are conducted by Zoom under the court's remote-proceedings procedures.

Electronic filing and pro se intake

The District of Kansas uses CM/ECF for case filing. Attorneys file electronically; pro se debtors typically file paper petitions at one of the three divisional clerk's offices, although the court also offers Electronic Filing for Self-Represented Litigants (EFSS) for certain post-petition documents. The court's Local Bankruptcy Rules govern formatting, signatures, and chambers procedures.

For required schedules and statements, see the national Official Forms set (B 101 voluntary petition, B 106 schedules, B 107 statement of financial affairs, B 122 means-test forms).

Required pre-filing steps

  • Credit counseling certificate — required under 11 U.S.C. § 109(h) within 180 days before filing. See approved providers reference.
  • Means test (Chapter 7) — required for individual consumer cases. See means-test reference.
  • Exemption schedule (Schedule C) — Kansas is an opt-out state; filers must use Kansas state exemptions. See Kansas exemptions reference.
  • Tax returns — copies of the most recent federal tax return must be supplied to the trustee under 11 U.S.C. § 521(e)(2).
Each filed case is assigned to one of the bankruptcy judges of the District. See the roster of judges for primary-source records, and the trustees reference for the structure of trustee assignments by chapter.
